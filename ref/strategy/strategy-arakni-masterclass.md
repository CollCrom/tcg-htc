# Masterclass: Arakni, Marionette — Strategy Guide

**Source:** fabtcg.com/articles/masterclass-arakni
**Author:** Yuki Lee Bender (pro player)
**Context:** Post-Armory Deck: Arakni release, post Orb-Weaver Spinneret (Blue + Yellow) ban

---

## Overview

Arakni, Marionette is an aggressive deck with attack reactions and a sprinkle of disruption. The combination of dagger pumps and disruptive stealth finishers keeps opponents guessing and makes defending extremely challenging. Arakni has a robust card pool and can adjust to target any metagame.

## Mark Your Prey

Marking is central to Arakni's gameplan. Key marking sources:
- **Hunter's Klaive** + **Flick Knives** — onboard marking
- **Up Sticks and Run**, **Pick Up the Point** — additional daggers
- **Scar Tissue**, **Cut from the Same Cloth**, **Relentless Pursuit**, **Reaper's Call**, **Lair of the Spider** — mark effects in deck

### Value of Marking (~1.5 value on average)
- +1 power on stealth attacks (hero ability)
- Go again on hit (hero ability)
- Agent of Chaos transformation at end of turn
- **Graphene Chelicera** gets go again vs marked
- **Savor Bloodshed** draws a card on next dagger hit vs marked
- **Mark of the Black Widow** forces opponent to lose a card from hand

### Marking Strategy
- Don't flick Klaive just because you have a mark payoff — marking is a limited resource
- Look for a few high-value mark opportunities per game
- Avoid flicking last Hunter's Klaive until late game (sub-15 life)
- First Klaive can be flicked aggressively (opens weapon slot for Chelicera)

## Agents of Chaos

Three categories:

### Category 1: Universal Assassin buffs (Black Widow, Funnel Web, Redback)
- Discard any Assassin card as attack reaction: +3 power to any Assassin attack
- Bonus effects on stealth attacks
- **Black Widow** (best) — on-hit banish card from opponent's hand
- **Redback** — go again, good at low life for unpredictability
- **Funnel Web** (narrowest) — on-hit banish card from opponent's arsenal

### Category 2: Narrow but efficient (Orb-Weaver, Tarantula)
- **Orb-Weaver** — generates Graphene Chelicera + free swing, buffs stealth attacks. One of the most powerful forms.
- **Tarantula** — buffs dagger attacks, life loss on dagger damage adds up. Threatening at low life.

### Category 3: Defensive (Trap-Door)
- Search deck, banish face-down, play if trap
- Generates ~3 extra defense value
- One of the most powerful agents alongside Black Widow and Orb-Weaver
- Downside: next turn weak offensively (unless Mask of Deceit re-transforms)

## Dagger Management

### Ideal Setup: 1 Klaive + 1 Chelicera
- Klaive (cost 2, power 1, go again, marks on hit) + Chelicera (cost 1, power 1, stealth)
- Together: 3 resources for 3 power with marking and go again
- Pitchless Chelicera turns: 2-card-9 (extremely efficient)

### Chelicera Benefits
- Stealth typing: +1 power if marked (Marionette), on-hits from agents, Stains of the Redback target
- Tarantula Toxin: +3 power AND -3 defense = 0-for-6 attack reaction
- Hard to defend due to Tarantula Toxin threat

### Kiss of Death Synergies
- Stealth + dagger typing, 0-for-3 with lose 1 life on-hit
- Can be flicked by Flick Knives for bonus 2 damage (1 from flick + 1 from on-hit re-trigger)
- Black Widow combo: give Kiss on-hit banish from hand, give go again, flick on next chain link for double on-hit triggers

### Flick Knives Strategy
- First Klaive: flick aggressively to open weapon slot for Chelicera
- Sequence Orb-Weaver Spinneret turns: flick existing Chelicera, then make new one
- Don't accidentally unmark opponent when you have Savor Bloodshed or Mark of the Black Widow

## Stealth vs Dagger Focus

### Stealth-Heavy
- Excels vs decks that dislike defending (Cindra, Oscilio, Dash)
- Key cards: Meet Madness, Mark of the Black Widow, Art of Desire: Body
- Additional reactions: Razor's Edge, Night's Embrace, Take Up the Mantle

### Dagger-Heavy
- Excels vs defensive decks (Verdance, Kassai, Pleiades)
- Key cards: Savor Bloodshed, Tarantula Toxin, Up Sticks and Run, Cut from the Same Cloth
- 0-for-4 rate is very efficient, hard to defend without defense reactions
- Supplemented by Sigil of Solace (bluffs a reaction)

### The Mix
- Having both is complementary — forces opponents to guess wrong on defense
- Dagger attack: do they defend (risking reactions) or hold back (risking stealth finisher)?

## Trap Strategy

- Always run at least 2 traps (for Trap-Door agent value)
- Up to 4 for Under the Trap-Door synergy (blue that doubles as red)

| Trap | Best Against |
|------|-------------|
| **Inertia Trap** | Nearly universal — attacks with power > base. Incredible value swing if they lose arsenal card |
| **Frailty Trap** | Go again + weapon attacks (mirror, Kassai). Can stop 5+ damage |
| **Lair of the Spider** / **Den of the Spider** | Go again decks (Ninjas, Mechanologists, Warriors). Keeps opponent marked |

## Playing Against Arakni (from Cindra's perspective)

- Don't defend Klaive, avoid defending Kiss of Death / Chelicera (Tarantula Toxin threat)
- Defend pumped Klaive swings or disruptive stealth finishers
- As aggro deck: send your own on-hits (Arakni runs lots of 2-defense)
- Defense reactions are extremely valuable — let you safely fight over on-hits
- Watch what they defend with and pitch to read their follow-ups
- Try to stay unmarked when possible
- Be aware of Inertia Trap when arsenaling + attacking above base power
- Equipment is best saved as contingency for disruption, not proactive defense

## Metagame Position

- Strong vs aggressive decks that don't defend (Cindra, Oscilio, Dash)
- Has tools to push through defensive decks (Verdance, Kassai)
- Main predator: Gravy Bones (Chelicera/hero ability care about "defending hero", hard to clear allies efficiently)
- Strength: can adjust deck to target any metagame
