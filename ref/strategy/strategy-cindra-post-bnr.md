# Cindra Post-BNR Updates — Redline vs Blue Cindra

**Source:** Community article
**Context:** After Brand with Cinderclaw and Wrath of Retribution banned. Exploring two rebuild paths.

---

## Impact of Bans

### Brand with Cinderclaw loss
- Lowers starter count
- Harder to reach 3 Draconic links for dagger retrieval
- Compounding effect: taxes Fealty more → further taxes link count and starter access
- Being stuck without daggers spirals quickly

### Wrath of Retribution loss
- Removes a guaranteed powerful turn in longer games
- Especially felt in slower matchups (Florian, Jarl)
- Lowers the deck's ceiling

### Core still strong
- Flick Knives + Mask of Momentum with unlimited daggers is very powerful
- Blood Splattered Vest remains an extremely powerful chest piece
- Cindra should still be top ~6-8 deck

## Path 1: Redline Cindra (Post-BNR)

**Decklist:** fabrary.net/decks/01KCSXVMAMPN2Y4RMXTAZ55W8R

### Approach
- Stay all-red, build heavily into **Oath of Loyalty** to maintain starter count
- Heavy Fealty generation: 3x Hunt the Hunter, 3x Display Loyalty, 3x Demonstrate Devotion
- Lower Fealty payoffs (no Art of Dragon: Fire or Scale)
- Only Snatch as non-Draconic chain ender
- Fealty used to smooth Oath turns and reach 3 Draconic links consistently

### Trade-offs
- Smooths out deck but at lower rate than pre-ban
- Can't replace Wrath of Retribution's ceiling
- Loses several points of damage over a game (no Art of Dragon: Fire, occasional pitch to Kunai)
- Open question: how many non-Draconic cards can we play while keeping Fealty economy healthy?

### 8 Defense Reaction "Furnace" Plan
- Good for longer games vs decks that don't block (Marionette, Cindra mirror, Kayo going first)
- Gives up points vs Gravy Bones and Earth heroes
- Worth the trade-off depends on expected metagame

## Path 2: Blue Cindra

**Decklist:** fabrary.net/decks/01KCJEX44JFJPXNQ0J101ZYA8E
**Reference:** Kinga Piper Top 4 Calling Hamburg, won Danish Nationals

### Approach
- Blues function as Draconic starters (pitch blue → swing Kunai)
- Enables higher count of 1-cost cards
- Opens up: Spreading Flames, Demonstrate Devotion, Mark with Magma, Dragon Power
- Saves deck slots on tech cards (Warmonger's)
- Free access to Overcrowded (for Marionette matchup) and Authority of Ataya (for Earth/Kassai)

### Strengths
- **Much higher ceiling turns**: multiple 1-for-7 finishers + big Spreading Flames turns
- Maximizes both Fealty generators AND payoffs (Dragon Fire + Dragon Power = 1-for-7s)
- Makes up for Wrath of Retribution loss through ceiling turns
- Very high Draconic starter density (Oath + Spreading + blues)

### Weaknesses
- **Extremely reliant on Blood Splattered Vest** — can never play anything else in chest
- Harder to sideboard defense reactions (resource curve matters more)
- Weak to dagger removal (Sunken Treasure from Gravy Bones) and Frailty tokens
- Blues + 1-costs lower consistency (the "too many 1-costs, no blue" problem like Ira)
- Sideboard needs work (room for 3 more cards, unclear what tech to play)

### Key Design Tension
- Average pitch value lower than redline
- Made up by: maxing Fealty generators, high density of Fealty payoffs (1-for-7s)
- Building a resource curve is important — can't just jam 1-costs randomly

## Comparison

| Aspect | Redline | Blue Cindra |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Consistency | Higher | Lower (blue/1-cost ratio) |
| Ceiling | Lower (no Wrath replacement) | Higher (Spreading Flames, multiple 1-for-7s) |
| Starter density | Needs Oath + Fealty | High (Oath + Spreading + blues) |
| Vest dependency | Important but not essential | Essential (can never replace) |
| Dagger vulnerability | Lower | Higher (swings daggers more) |
| Defense reactions | 8 dreacts feasible | 5-6 feasible, 8 tough |
| Fealty usage | Smooth Oath turns | Aggressive payoffs |
| Tech slots | Tighter | More flexible (saves Warmonger's slot) |
