# Masterclass: Deckbuilding (Yuki Lee Bender)
Source: https://fabtcg.com/articles/masterclass-deckbuilding/

## Overview

Deckbuilding is fluid and iterative. Steps aren't visited once in order — jump around, revisit with new knowledge. The process covers day-1 building with a new hero through to refined 80-card tournament decks.

## Step 1: What Does the Hero and Weapon Ask of You?

Each hero wants certain types/colors/properties of cards based on their textbox and weapons:

- **Verdance**: Earth cards, decompose, life gain. Staff of Verdant Shoots and Waning Moon want non-attack actions.
- **Kayo**: Cards with 6+ power (5+ with his static ability).
- **Arakni**: Ways to mark, attacks with stealth for +1 power. Tarantula wants dagger swings, Trap-Door wants traps. Hunter's Klaive marks but 2 resources for 1 damage is inefficient alone.
- **Aurora**: Lightning cards for Star Fall and hero ability.
- **Fang**: Fealty tokens and/or marks, Draconic cards for daggers.
- **Gravy Bones**: Blue cards with go again, allies, ways to make Gold or discard allies.

## Step 2: How Does Equipment Inform Deckbuilding?

Equipment starts in play — some are just defensive value, others are build-arounds:

- **Minor build-arounds**: Storm Striders (Verdance) — asks for some arcane damage spells. Gauntlets of Tyrannical Rex (Kayo) — justifies higher blue count.
- **Major build-arounds**: Flick Knives (Arakni) — marks on demand, wants Graphene Chelicera generation. Fyendal's Spring Tunic — needs planned resource curve outlets. Snapdragon Scalers — enables specific Black Widow + Kiss of Death + Flick sequences.
- **Savage Sash** (Kayo) — enables 4-5 attack wide combat chains, especially with Bloodrush Bellow.

## Step 3: Card Evaluation

FaB is very mathematical. Quantify card power numerically.

### Baseline Attack Values
- Vanilla attacks: Wounding Blow (0 cost, 4 power, 3 defense) → +1 resource = +1 power
- These baselines aren't quite CC-playable. CC staples offer this efficiency + upside (Leave No Witnesses, Command and Conquer).
- **1 point above rate** = generally playable (Cadaverous Tilling, Wounded Bull)
- **2+ points above rate** = power outlier, almost auto-include (Show of Strength)

### Baseline Go Again Values
- Head Jab: 3 power, 2 defense, go again (1 less power than Wounding Blow for go again)
- 0-for-4 go again = 1 above rate, widely playable in aggressive CC decks (Blaze Headlong, Ravenous Rabble, Pouncing Qi)
- Wild Ride (2-for-6 + Might + go again) = 2 points above Surging Strike baseline

### Baseline Defense Values
- Cards defend for 3 baseline
- Defense reactions blocking 4 for 0 cost (Sink Below, Fate Foreseen) = 1 point above rate on defense

### Key Heuristic
- **Card draw ≈ 3 value** (from cards defending 3 / Head Jab attacking 3)
- Mental Block: prevents 2 damage + draws a card ≈ 5 value, significantly above rate

### Hand Value Baselines
- Titan's Fist / Autumn's Touch / Thunder Quake off blues: 1 card = 4 damage, 2 cards = 7, 3 cards = 10
- Each card worth ~3, action point worth ~1
- **Most hands should be ~13 points combined offense + defense to be on-rate**
- Meta decks approach **14-16 points per hand**
- 4-card plays pushing **16+ are very above rate** — decks try to create a few per game

## Step 4: Identify Power Plays

Look for power outliers — individual cards or combinations significantly above rate:

- **Codex of Frailty** (Arakni): Card of choice + Ponder + Frailty = 2 cards for 1 + Frailty. Codex → Leave No Witnesses = ~7-8 value from 1 card.
- **Savor Bloodshed + Kiss of Death** (marked): 2-card 8 with draw + go again on-hit. Add Tarantula Toxin = potential 3-card 15 + draw + go again.
- **Bloodrush Bellow** (Kayo): 1 resource + 2 cards → draw 2 + +2 power all turn. Replaces itself (~3 value), +2 effect worth 4-6 extra if 2-3 links wide. Total ~8-10 value with go again for 1 resource.
- **Felling of the Crown** (Verdance): 2-card 8 with decompose + takes a card from opponent = 2-card 11.
- **3 Rampant Growth + 3 Healing Potions** (Verdance): 33 damage + 9 life = 6-card 42 (but requires massive setup time).

## Step 5: Building a Resource Curve

Blues are resource cards — build to consistently spend all pitched resources off 1-2 blues:

- **Verdance**: 3-cost cards (no easy go again for 1-2 costs), or 1-cost non-attacks + Waning Moon for remaining 2.
- **Kayo**: 2-cost attacks for attack-claw-attack patterns.
- **Arakni**: Hunter's Klaive costs 2. Play 2-costs with Tunic, 1-costs without. 0-cost attacks/reactions fit when swinging Klaive + Chelicera.
- **Prism**: Pitch yellows for Luminaris go again. 2-cost Heralds + 4-cost auras. Wartune Herald at 1-cost fits curve.
- **Aurora**: Star Fall + hero ability uses full blue → favors 0-cost cards.

### How Many Blues?

| Blues needed/hand | Approximate range | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0-3 | Cindra |
| 0-1 | 6-10 | Slippy, Aurora |
| 1 | 15-21 | Arakni, Ira, Dash I/O, Fai, Kassai, Dorinthea |
| 1-2 | 22-27 | Kayo, Verdance, Florian |
| 2+ | 30+ | Gravy Bones, Guardians, Zen, Enigma, Nuu |

**Key insight**: Decks with extremely low OR extremely high blue counts are often very powerful due to extra consistency. Worth exploring if a hero can build in either direction.

## Step 6: Glue Cards and Consistency

Strongest decks have many redundant pieces performing similar roles. Glue cards aren't power outliers but advance the main gameplan consistently.

## Step 7: Finding a Direction

Common build directions:
- **Defense reactions** — for decks with powerful small hands
- **Disruption/midrange** — for smaller hands wanting longer games
- **Aggressive/go again** — converting full hands offensively at expense of defense

Start with a focused 60-card deck with a singular plan. Sum up what the deck does in 1-2 sentences (thesis statement). Only include cards that contribute to this gameplan.

## Step 8: Test Drive

- **Goldfish** proactive/offensive decks (draw and play hands without opponent) for rapid iteration
- Evaluate value per turn (combined block + attack). Target **14+ average**.
- Look for cards you're happy/unhappy to see
- Watch for consistent patterns, not one-off variance
- Check: too many/few blues? Floating resources? Tunic staying at 3 counters? Trouble completing deck's goal?

## Step 9: Troubleshoot Specific Matchups

Play base 60 into each meta deck first to assess baseline. Look for:
- **Value advantage** → outvalue turn over turn
- **Value disadvantage vs aggro** → add disruption/defense
- **Value disadvantage vs defense** → set up 5-card hands, deny defensive value
- **On-hit problems** → defense reactions, equipment, or gameplay adjustments
- **Specific tech cards** → tools that line up into opponent's strongest plays

**Build ideal 60-card decks for each important matchup.** Compare configurations — cards in all matchups form the "core." Total needed cards across all configurations = starting point for 80.

Try extreme approaches to find what works: full offense, alternating 3/5 card hands, always blocking on-hits, full fatigue defense.

## Step 10: Sideboard Strategy

### Equipment First
Equipment is higher impact per sideboard slot than maindeck cards because:
- Only takes 1 slot
- Starts in play (always available, unlike a defense reaction you might never draw)

### Blue Tech Cards
Blue tech cards with 3 defense can live in the main resource base at minimal cost (Overcrowded, Warmonger's Diplomacy, Condemn to Slaughter, Fearless Confrontation). This frees sideboard slots for high-impact reds and equipment.

### Building the 80
- Start with 85-90 cards, gradually trim
- Evaluate each card by: (1) how many matchups it's played in, (2) how much it improves win rate, (3) expected meta share of decks it's for
- **Look for overlap** — cards useful in multiple matchups are premium sideboard slots
- Theoretical value: win_rate_increase × meta_share × num_matchups

### Core vs Sideboard
The "core" is simply cards played in all matchups. The "sideboard" is cards that move in/out. This distinction is descriptive, not prescriptive — don't treat any card as too "sacred" to cut. Example: some pros sideboarded out Rain Razors in the Lexi mirror despite it being "core" because the matchup prioritized defending on-hits and small hands.

**Think of your deck as 80 cards that let you play many different 60-card decks**, not as a core + sideboard.

## Key Principles

1. Deckbuilding is iterative — revisit earlier steps with new knowledge
2. Make small changes systematically, one problem at a time
3. A card worth 3 defense/offense is baseline; 1+ above rate is CC-playable; 2+ is a power outlier
4. Meta decks target 14-16 value per hand
5. Resource curve efficiency matters — don't leave resources floating
6. Equipment is the highest-impact sideboard tech
7. Blue tech cards can live in the main resource base cheaply
8. Don't be limited by "core" vs "sideboard" — every card is cuttable in the right matchup
9. Re-evaluate your 80 for each tournament even if close together
