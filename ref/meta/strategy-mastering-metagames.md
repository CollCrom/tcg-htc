## Five-Step Metagame Prediction Framework

### Step 1: Analyze Recent Results

Look at recent big events for:
- What decks won the most?
- What decks were played the most?
- Trends in gaining/losing popularity vs previous weeks?
- Is there a clear best deck or group of best decks?

**Apply logic and reasoning** to form a narrative about why decks are excelling or struggling. But remember: a single event or single week is low sample size — could be variance/noise. Look for trends across multiple weeks.

**Community sentiment** (especially from content creators) can inform predictions — popular consensus often matches what shows up next event. But always verify with your own testing.

### Step 2: Identify Decks Losing Meta Share

Look for decks that are poorly positioned into what did well recently. When a major player falls out, it can enable other decks to rise.

**Key insight**: Even decks losing popularity won't disappear entirely. Dedicated players stick with their heroes, and if the field stops teching against them, they can resurge.

### Step 3: Identify Decks Gaining Meta Share

Look for decks with good matchups into top-performing decks, especially those that had bad matchups into decks now falling out of favor. Heroes can be pushed out by meta forces, then become strong once things shift.

### Step 4: Estimate How Much the Meta Will Shift

Factors:
- **Quantity of decks** changing in popularity
- **Which decks** are in question — some heroes have outsized influence on the meta (gatekeepers)
- **Time until event** — players need time to acquire cards and learn new decks. Events a week out shift less than events a month out.

If decks losing popularity gatekeep other decks, expect bigger meta shakeups.

### Step 5: Deck Selection and Meta Calls

With predicted meta in hand, decide:
- **Play a top deck** — safe, proven choice
- **Make a meta call** — target the top deck(s) with favorable matchups, accepting weaker matchups elsewhere

Meta call requirements:
- Solid matchups into the 1-2 most represented decks
- At least some game into the rest of the field
- Don't sacrifice too much in other matchups — even the most represented deck isn't your only opponent

## Applied Analysis: Pro Tour Singapore Meta (July 2025)

### Current State
- **Gravy Bones, Cindra, Slippy**: 4 Nationals wins each
- **Verdance, Prism**: 3 wins each
- No clear single best deck — diverse and healthy

### Meta Dynamics

**Cindra** rose as a counter to Gravy Bones (splits damage to clear allies, Mask of Momentum draws, heavy pressure shortens Gravy's setup time). Expected to grow in popularity.

**Slippy** has easy go again + disruption to pressure Gravy while managing allies. Assassin class plays favorably into low-defense decks like Cindra. Good into Verdance and Prism too. Not clear what bad matchups Slippy has in current meta.

**Gravy Bones** is clearly powerful but only a month old — lists continue to refine. Expected most represented at Pro Tour. Weakness: allies vulnerable to on-hit effects (especially Ninja class).

**Prism** looks solid on paper but weakness to poppers is exploitable. Many top lists increasing popper density. Expected to dip slightly.

**Verdance** powerful with broad game but struggles with Gravy's allies and Cindra matchup isn't favored. Expected to drop in popularity unless adaptations found.

### Predicted Meta for Pro Tour
1. Gravy Bones and Cindra — most represented
2. Slippy — close third
3. Prism — moderate representation
4. Verdance — lesser representation

### Dark Horse Picks

**Dash I/O**: Races aggressive Gravy counters, exploits Gravy's slow setup with Boom Grenades, easy poppers for Prism. Traditional Earth weakness less relevant with Verdance/Florian declining. Risk: Slippy/Assassin matchup.

**Kassai**: Gains Copper off Gravy's allies for Blood on Her Hands. Solid into Cindra and Slippy. Main predators (Wizards, Prism) potentially declining. Opened up by Enigma hitting Living Legend.

**Ira**: Mask of Momentum strong vs allies. New Iris of the Blossom searches Whirling Mist Blossom to draw 2 against allies. Already solid Gravy gameplan before new support.

**Boltyn**: Solid Gravy matchup, favorable Cindra and Prism matchups, competent vs Verdance and Assassin. Weakness: Guardians and Warriors (not currently popular). Under-explored.

## Key Metagame Principles

1. **Sample size matters** — don't overreact to single events, look for multi-week trends
2. **Gatekeepers shape the meta** — when they fall, everything downstream shifts
3. **Time delays adaptation** — the further out an event, the more the meta will adjust
4. **Meta calls require breadth** — targeting one deck at the cost of all others is rarely correct
5. **Dedicated players persist** — never assume a hero is completely gone from the meta
6. **Narratives need verification** — logical-sounding explanations for results could still be noise
7. **Win rate isn't the only metric** — play rate, Top 8 rate, and representation all matter
