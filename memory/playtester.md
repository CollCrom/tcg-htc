## Strategic Learnings

- Recognize opponent over-blocking patterns early to inform arsenal choices — keep finishers in hand when opponents burn resources on starters
- Block meaningful attacks (3+ damage) with high-defense cards, not 1-damage pokes; blocking 1 damage with a 4-defense card wastes 3 points of defensive value
- Equipment activation costs require early turn resource planning — pitch blues early to float resources for later activations
- Attack reactions that push damage through committed blocks extract maximum value at zero cost
- Commit to equipment-enabled chain extensions before the first attack resolves, not after it's already been blocked — activating go-again on a blocked chain link generates zero value
- When an opponent is at low life and you hold go-again starters plus finishers, extending the chain is usually correct over saving for "a stronger next turn"
- Disruption tools (e.g., Warmonger's Diplomacy Peace mode) have meaningful value even with an all-blue hand — don't pass entire turns when disruption is available and opponent is near lethal
- Sink Below in arsenal is a free 4-defense reaction; always account for it before taking damage

## User Corrections

- **Only activate Dragonscaler Flight Path when the chain extension leads to meaningful damage.** Destroying your equipment and spending resources to grant go-again is only worth it if you have a strong follow-up ready to capitalize on the extra action point.
- **Warmonger's Diplomacy: each hero's choice restricts THEMSELVES, not the opponent.** Choosing Peace means YOU can only play non-attack non-weapon actions next turn. Choosing War means YOU can only play weapons and attack actions. As Cindra (the aggressor), almost always choose War — Peace locks you out of attacking. The opponent's restriction comes from their own choice, not yours.

## Hero Notes

#### Cindra
- Open with go-again cards + Flick Knives activation to establish Blood Splattered Vest engine early
- Dragonscaler Flight Path requires proactive resource management — plan 2-resource turns by pitching blues early; failure to do so makes it a dead equipment piece
- Arsenal go-again starters (Enflame, Breaking Point) and high-power finishers to enable multi-card offensive turns
- Warmonger's Diplomacy "Peace" mode is generally better than "War" against weapon-primary heroes like Arakni; War mode restricts weapon attacks while Peace restricts action cards — choose based on what the opponent is threatening that specific turn, not by default
- Fealty activation is best timed when opponent has exhausted blocks and the power boost closes lethal — don't waste it on attacks that will be blocked
- Stacking multiple free attack reactions (e.g., double Ancestral Empowerment) on a single attack is strong value when resources are tight and opponent blocks are limited

#### Arakni
- Hunter's Klaive remains the primary threat even when restricted by Warmonger's Diplomacy "War" mode
- Tends to over-block low-power attacks, creating value extraction opportunities for opponents — recognize this pattern and pass rather than extending chains once value is extracted
- 1-damage Klaive swings should generally not be blocked with cards unless a Marked condition makes denying contract bonuses worthwhile

## Matchup Notes

#### Cindra vs Arakni
- Arakni frequently over-commits cards to block starter attacks — exploit this by passing after extracting value rather than extending chains
- War mode on Warmonger's Diplomacy doesn't meaningfully restrict Arakni's weapon-based gameplan; Peace mode is usually the stronger default choice
- Sink Below is particularly valuable in this matchup for denying Marked contract bonuses on weapon swings — the 4-defense and card cycling justify spending it even on low-damage hits when Marked is active
- Dragonscaler Flight Path go-again chains are a key win condition; resource-plan for the 2-cost activation from turn 1 or the equipment will sit idle
