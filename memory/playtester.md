## Strategic Learnings

- Recognize opponent over-blocking patterns early to inform arsenal choices — keep finishers in hand when opponents burn resources on starters
- Block meaningful attacks (3+ damage) with high-defense cards, not 1-damage pokes
- Equipment activation costs require early turn resource planning — pitch blues early to float resources for later activations
- Attack reactions that push damage through committed blocks extract maximum value at zero cost

## 3. Mistakes

**T5 — Blocking 1 damage with Shelter from the Storm (4 defense)**
The log explicitly passes on blocking the 5-power attack to preserve hand, then uses Shelter to block 1 damage. This is contradictory and incorrect. Shelter from the Storm should block meaningful attacks (3+ damage) or be pitched offensively. Blocking 1 damage with it wastes 3 points of defensive value.

**T6, T8, T10, T12 — Repeated Dragonscaler non-activation on blocked chain links**
Dragonscaler Flight Path was almost never activated throughout the game. While many individual passes were correct (can't afford 2 resources, attack already blocked), the pattern suggests the equipment was underutilized. A core piece of the kit should be generating value more frequently — this likely required better resource planning earlier in turns to enable the activation.

**T28 — Passing on extending the chain with Breaking Point + Art of Dragon: Blood**
With Breaking Point in arsenal and Art of Dragon: Blood in hand (go-again, 4 power), passing the chain after Display Loyalty hit was conservative. The reasoning cited "saving for a stronger next turn" but the opponent was at 19 HP — extending here with two more attacks (4+5=9 damage) could have accelerated the close significantly.

**T34 — Warmonger's Diplomacy "War" mode at 8 HP opponent**
Choosing War locks the opponent into weapon/attack actions, but Arakni's primary threat IS weapons (Hunter's Klaive). War mode doesn't meaningfully restrict Arakni's gameplan and may have been the wrong choice — Peace would have prevented them from attacking back, which is more relevant when you're trying to survive to close out.

## 4. Patterns

**Dragonscaler Flight Path was a dead equipment piece most of the game.** The 2-resource activation cost was consistently cited as the reason not to use it, but the deck's resource generation (Blood Splattered Vest + Flick Knives) exists precisely to fund these activations. Better planning — pitching a blue early in a turn to float resources for Dragonscaler — would unlock the go-again chains the equipment is designed for.

**Opponent over-blocking was a recurring gift.** Multiple turns (T8: 4 cards for Enflame 2p; T10: 4 cards for Mark with Magma) saw the opponent massively over-commit. Recognizing this pattern earlier could have informed more aggressive arsenal choices — keeping finishers in hand rather than arsenal when the opponent is likely to burn resources on starters.

**Arsenal management was strong.** Consistently arsenaling go-again starters (Enflame, Breaking Point) and high-power finishers (Art of Dragon: Fire) to set up multi-card offensive turns.
